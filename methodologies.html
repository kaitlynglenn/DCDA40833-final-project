Methodologies

This project uses a combination of survey data, academic research, and media sources to explore how TikTok shapes everyday culture. I collected responses about users’ experiences with identity, consumer behavior, politics, subcultures, and parasocial relationships, then compared those results with existing studies and articles about TikTok’s social impact. Together, these methods help show both personal experiences and broader cultural patterns connected to TikTok use.

Main Discussion

Core Points

A Combination of Digital Culture and Data Analytics

For this project, I used a mix of both digitcal culture and data analytics, but focused mostly on digital culture and the effects that Tik Tok has on it. I used data analytics to collect survey responses from TikTok users about their experiences with identity, consumer behavior, politics, subcultures, and parasocial relationships. I then analyzed those responses to identify common themes and patterns in how TikTok influences users’ thoughts and behaviors. I also used digital culture research to find academic studies and media articles that explore similar topics, which helped me connect the survey results to broader cultural trends and understand the significance of TikTok’s impact on everyday life.

AI's Help in Creating the Deliverable

I used AI to help create the deliverable by generating a good outline to copy and paste into my htmls that I created. This was extremely helpful because it gave me a clear structure to follow for each page, which made it easier to organize my research and present my findings in a coherent way. It generated the perfect font and color scheme for my project to match the aesthetic of TikTok, which made the design process much easier and more efficient. Overall, using AI saved me a lot of time and effort in creating the deliverable, allowing me to focus more on the content and research rather than the technical aspects of building the website.

Survey

To retrieve the information I wanted out of this, I knew I could not rely soley on research, but needed to get some real life data from TikTok users. I created a survey that asked questions about how TikTok has influenced their identity, consumer behavior, political awareness, involvement in online communities, and parasocial relationships. I shared the survey on social media and through personal networks to gather responses from a diverse group of TikTok users. The survey results provided valuable insights into how people experience TikTok’s influence in their daily lives, which helped me connect the academic research to real-world experiences and make my project more relatable and impactful.

Findings

When starting this project, I had a list of topics that I wanted to explore, but I knew it would be too much and would blow my project out of proportion. I decided to focus on five main topics: identity, consumer behavior, politics, subcultures, and parasocial relationships. These topics were chosen because they represent key areas where TikTok has a significant influence on users’ lives and culture. By focusing on these specific areas, I was able to dive deeper into the research and provide more detailed insights into how TikTok shapes everyday experiences in these domains.
