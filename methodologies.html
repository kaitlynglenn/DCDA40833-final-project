Methodologies

This project uses a combination of survey data, academic research, and media sources to explore how TikTok shapes everyday culture. I collected responses about users’ experiences with identity, consumer behavior, politics, subcultures, and parasocial relationships, then compared those results with existing studies and articles about TikTok’s social impact. Together, these methods help show both personal experiences and broader cultural patterns connected to TikTok use.

Main Discussion

Core Points

A Combination of Digital Culture and Data Analytics

One of the biggest things I learned from this project is how strongly TikTok’s algorithm shapes users’ identities and everyday decisions. Through sections on beauty standards, subcultures, and parasocial relationships, I saw how repeated exposure to certain content influences how people present themselves and connect with others online. The survey results also supported this idea by showing that many users recognize TikTok’s impact on their appearance, interests, and opinions.

AI's Help in Creating the Deliverable

Another important takeaway from this project is that TikTok can be both useful and limiting as a source of information. While it helps users discover political issues, communities, and new products quickly, the platform’s short-form content and personalized algorithm can also create bias or encourage impulse decisions. This made me realize that TikTok plays a powerful role in shaping digital culture, but users need to think critically about the content they see.

Why It Matters

Importance

Understanding how TikTok shapes digital culture is important because many users interact with the platform every day without realizing how much it influences their thoughts, preferences, and decisions. By learning about its effects on identity, consumer behavior, political awareness, subcultures, and relationships with creators, this project helps raise awareness of how algorithm-driven content shapes everyday experiences online. Recognizing these influences encourages users to think more critically about their TikTok usage and make more intentional choices about the content they watch, trust, and engage with.
