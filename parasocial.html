Section 05

Parasocial Relationships

This page explores how TikTok encourages users to develop parasocial relationships with influencers and celebrities through repeated exposure to personal and relatable content. Because creators often share everyday moments, opinions, and routines, viewers can feel a sense of connection even without direct interaction. These one-sided relationships can influence how users think, behave, and make decisions online. This section connects to the overall project by showing how TikTok’s algorithm shapes not only what people watch, but also how they form emotional connections and social expectations in digital culture.

Evidence

Survey Results

These results show that many users follow creators whose opinions influence their thoughts or decisions, highlighting how common parasocial relationships are on TikTok. Because viewers regularly see creators’ personal content, advice, and recommendations, they can begin to trust them in ways similar to real-life relationships.

These results show that many users feel at least somewhat connected to the creators they watch regularly, demonstrating how easily parasocial relationships can develop on TikTok. Even moderate or slight feelings of connection can influence how viewers trust creators’ opinions, recommendations, and lifestyles. This supports the idea that repeated exposure to personal content helps creators feel relatable and familiar, strengthening their impact on users’ attitudes and decisions in digital culture.

Why It Matters

Importance

This section matters to my larger research question because it shows how TikTok’s algorithm shapes not only what users watch, but also how they form emotional connections with creators online. By encouraging repeated exposure to influencers’ personal content and opinions, the platform makes it easier for users to develop trust and familiarity with people they have never met. These parasocial relationships can influence beliefs, behaviors, and decisions, supporting the broader argument that TikTok plays a powerful role in shaping identity and everyday experiences in digital culture.
