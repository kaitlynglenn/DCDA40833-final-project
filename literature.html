Literature Review

This literature review examines existing research on how TikTok influences culture, identity, consumer behavior, political awareness, online communities, and parasocial relationships. By bringing together academic studies and media analyses, this section explores what scholars and researchers already understand about TikTok’s social impact, as well as the gaps that still exist in explaining how the platform shapes everyday experiences, especially for younger users.

"The Biggest Ways TikTok has Changed American Culture" by CNN

This CNN article is a broad overview of TikTok’s cultural impact in the United States. The article argues that TikTok has not just become a popular app, but has changed how people live, eat, watch, and buy. It frames TikTok as a force that affects multiple industries and everyday life, even for people who do not actively use the platform. That makes it especially useful as a starting point for my project, because it captures TikTok as a large cultural system rather than just a social media platform. It helps me explain why my topic matters: TikTok’s influence goes far beyond entertainment and has become part of everyday American culture. However, it does not give enough information for me to just use this article as my evidence to answer my research questions. I was able to take this and amplify it through research and surveys.

"Women Who Spend Time on TikTok Feel Less Satisfied with their Bodies, Study Suggests" by NBC

This NBC News article reports on research showing that TikTok use, especially exposure to pro-anorexia content, may worsen body image satisfaction in young women. The study reports that Australian researchers studied 273 women ages 18 to 28 and found that even brief exposure to harmful TikTok content could increase body dissatisfaction and internalization of beauty standards. I assessed this source as useful for my project because it translates academic findings into clear language for a broad audience, which makes it easier to use in a project aimed at educating people. However, as a news article, it simplifies the underlying research and should be paired with the original study when possible. This source is especially valuable for my project’s section on beauty standards and identity because it helps connect algorithm-driven content exposure to real emotional and psychological effects on users. Although it gives us all of this information, it leaves room for me to explore the topic further by gathering my own evidence through surveys and more in-depth research.
